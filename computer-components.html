💾 RAM — SHORT-TERM MEMORY

RAM vs Storage

💡 ANALOGY RAM = your desk workspace. Storage (SSD/HDD) = your filing cabinet. Your desk (RAM) holds what you're actively working on. When you close a program, it goes back in the cabinet (storage). More desk space = more programs open at once.

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES

What beginners get wrong

❌ MISTAKE 1Confusing RAM with storage. "My computer only has 8GB" — do you mean RAM or SSD? They're completely different things.
❌ MISTAKE 2Thinking more GHz always = faster. A 4-core 3.5GHz CPU often outperforms an 8-core 2.0GHz CPU for single-threaded tasks.
❌ MISTAKE 3Ignoring cooling. CPUs throttle when hot — slowing down to avoid damage. Good cooling is essential for sustained performance.
❌ MISTAKE 4Not checking RAM compatibility. DDR4 and DDR5 are not interchangeable. Check your motherboard's supported specs before buying.
💡 Quick Summary — Computer Hardware
CPU = processor (brain). Cores handle parallel work. GHz = speed. Cache = ultra-fast built-in memory.
RAM = temporary workspace. Fast but volatile (lost on power off). More = more apps open at once.
SSD = fast permanent storage. HDD = slow, cheap permanent storage.
Motherboard connects everything. Determines what CPU/RAM types you can use.
GPU handles graphics and parallel computing (also used for AI/ML workloads).
